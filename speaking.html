Who's speaking

Daniel Fox

Music Behaviorist

I came to this craft-first. I'm a working music producer and multi-instrumentalist, so I know sound from the chair where it gets made. I spent years on stages and in sessions, learning before I ever read a study how a room of strangers can move the same way at the same moment. Then I went looking for why. Now I study how music shapes customer behavior, and I read what the peer-reviewed work actually says, naming the authors, the journals, and the limits, before I make a claim from a stage. The retail floor is where I prove it, so on that turf I'm a Retail Music Behaviorist. I'm based in Denver, and I'm the founder and CEO of Entuned, where this turned into a business in 2024.

My argument is simple. Music is a language and a mastered pattern, and its effect on behavior can be designed on purpose. What I bring to a stage is the part most behavioral-science talks are missing: a practitioner who has sat in the chair.

Signature keynote

The Soul Myth: Why Music Is a Language, and Why That's Not Cold

Music isn't magic. It's mastered pattern. This talk makes the case that treating sound as a language doesn't diminish it; it's what lets us understand why a room full of strangers responds the same way. The most personal and the most provocative thing I argue, from the one chair that lets me argue it: a working musician's.

Signature talk

Designing for Permission: Moving People Without Manipulating Them

Good design removes the friction between a person and the thing they already want. It doesn't push them toward something they don't. That line is the whole ethic, and it's the line between this work and manipulation. I've watched it hold on the retail floor at Entuned, where the right sound gives a shopper permission to buy, and in public space at Dizzy Charlie's summer concerts, where it gives a crowd permission to move. The same respect for what a person came in wanting, from the shelf to the public square.
